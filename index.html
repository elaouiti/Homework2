10,000 TREES Redesign

Task

To redesign the 10,000 Trees website to increase user engagement and increase overall donations.

Goal

Our goal was to increase user engagement and motivation to donate especially during the pandemic as they were negatively impacted. We want to achieve this by changing the design, increasing the ‘donate’ call to action, and give motivation through a leader board and transparency of where the donations directly go.

My Role & Responsibilities

Project Manager and UI Designer Creating wireframes Prototyping Visual Design

Timeline

3 week timeline & what I worked on Week One: User research, conducting user interviews, card sorting Week Two: Wireframing, Prototyping, UI Design Style Guide Week Three: Applying style guide, A/B testing, iterating, final prototyping

​

THE PROCESS

What I did

In this research project, I planned and conducted 5 user interviews with individuals who have experience in volunteering or donating.

The Process

Wireframing was extremely important to my process because it helped me layout the foundations of the design, and mapped out what my team believed was essential for the homepage.

User Research & Findings

In this research project, I was the team manager making sure my team members assigned tasks were going according to plan and I assisted them in any way they needed. I was also in charge of wireframing and prototyping once we got to that stage of design.

Ideation Design

Creating our mid-fidelity wireframe for our homepage helped me further layout what my team felt was important to include on the home page. This is also where we started to think that we should start to make some changes to the layout with there being too much white space.

Usability Testing & Findings

We learned through user A/B testing that the navigation bar should be moved above rather than below or beside the logo. We also got feed back to liven up the page with more colour and for there to be more information about where the donation goes on the homepage. Thus, I made a few iterations and this was our final product.

Interactive Prototype

In order to complete the redesign, I also integrated our style guide and my redesign on the desktop website into a mobile version of this site. Normally I would begin with a mobile design to work with the least amount of space and then work up to the desktop. However, my team wanted to focus more on the desktop because the existing mobile didn’t need as much of a redesign as the desktop did.
